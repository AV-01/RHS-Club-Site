Frequently Asked Questions

How Do I Start A Club?

Starting a club is relatively simple. First, the obvious step; get an idea and, optionally, gather some friends to help you. An idea can be practically anything (school appropriate!)--campus has had a Johnny Depp Appreciation Club in the past.

Next, find an advisor. An advisor must be a staff member on campus. Look for someone who fits the club's topic well, and someone who you personally like!

Then, you'll need to create a club constitution. This constitution must contain a statement of purpose, a list of eligibility requirements, and a list of officers and their job descriptions.

  • A statement of purpose simply must capture what your club aims to do.
  • A list of eligibility requirements outlines the requirements to join the club. These do not have to be rigorous--for some clubs, "You must be interested in the subject matter" will probably suffice. For others, this might include GPA requirements, etc.
  • The list of officers outlines what positions of power exist in your club. These might include a president, treasurer, secretary, etc. Do not name specific students in this portion.
You can find a template constitution here.

You will also need to create an ASB account and deposit 25 dollars into it. These 25 dollars are not a fee; rather, they are the initial funds in your club's treasury, which you will be able to use later for necessary expenses.

Finally, have your advisor sign the constitution and turn it in to the office. Congratulations; upon acceptance, your club officially exists!


How Do I Run A Club?

The easiest way to advertise your club is at Club Rush, which takes place in the first few weeks after summer vacation. ASB will make a sign for you--go out there and claim a table for your club. Make sure to have some way to catalogue interested people-- maybe a Remind or a Discord server. Having some information and decoration on your table can't hurt, either.

From there, it very much depends on what your club is about. Organize meetings and events, leverage club funds, and have a good time!
